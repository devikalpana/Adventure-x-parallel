ADVENTURE




London's blend of historical landmarks such as Buckingham Palace and modern attractions like the London Eye draws millions each year. Rome's ancient ruins, including the Colosseum and Forum, alongside Vatican City's St. Peter's Basilica, offer a journey through centuries of history and culture. New York City's iconic skyline, Times Square's vibrant energy, and Central Park's tranquility define the quintessential urban experience. Tokyo's juxtaposition of ancient temples, neon-lit streets, and innovative technology showcases its dynamic spirit. Barcelona's architectural wonders by Gaudí, the bustling markets, and Mediterranean beaches capture the essence of vibrant Spanish culture. Bangkok's ornate temples, street markets brimming with exotic flavors, and a rich cultural tapestry make it a favorite for adventurous travelers.

BIKING




Mountain biking is trickier because you have to navigate hills and surfaces, so your upper body and core will kick into gear. It’s more of a total-body workout than biking on the road, which is mostly a lower-body cardio workout.Plan to get on your bike and ride for 30-60 minutes, 3-5 days a week

PARAGLIDING




Paragliding, sport of flying parachutes with design modifications that enhance their gliding capabilities. Unlike hang gliders, their close relations, paragliders have no rigid framework; the parachute canopy acts as a wing and is constructed of fabric cells with openings at the front that allow them to be inflated by movement through the air—the “ram-air” effect.

SURFING




Surfing’s roots lie in premodern Hawaii and Polynesia, where the sport was practiced by both men and women from all social strata from royalty to commoners. Early European explorers and travelers praised the skills of Hawaiian surfers, but 19th-century missionaries assigned to the islands disapproved of the “constant intermingling, without any restraint, of persons of both sexes” and banned the pastime
